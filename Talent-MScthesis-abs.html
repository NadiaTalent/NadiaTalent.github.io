Nadia Talent: MSc Thesis abstract


Talent, Nadia. Quantitative traits from leaf morphology in some North American species and hybrids of Populus sections Aigeiros and Tacamahaca (Salicaceae), MSc thesis, University of Toronto, Department of Botany, October 1998. (Supervisor: James E. Eckenwalder.)

This thesis is a preliminary step towards testing the genetic basis in poplars of quantitative leaf morphology traits that go beyond easily measured traits such as leaf length and width. The emphasis is on elliptic Fourier analysis of entire leaf outlinds, and a method is developed for graphical manipulation of distorted leaves that shows promise of reducing the noise factor that is generally troublesome with leaf samples. In the method, digital images of leaf halves are converted to a canonical shape by means of a geometric transformation known as "rubber-sheeting".

Some results are included concerning relationships between simple leaf-measurement variables in preformed and neoformed leaves from young trees. These relationships differ between trees in sections Aigeiros and Tacamahaca.
